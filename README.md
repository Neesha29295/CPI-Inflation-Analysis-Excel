# India CPI Inflation Analysis — Excel Project

## About
This project analyses India's Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation trends from 2013 to 2023 using Microsoft Excel. The analysis covers Rural, Urban, and Rural+Urban sectors across 27 product categories to identify major inflation drivers, seasonal patterns, and the impact of key economic events like COVID-19 and oil price fluctuations.

## Dataset
- **Source:** National Statistical Office (NSO) — CPI Dataset
- **Period:** 2013 — 2023
- **Sectors:** Rural, Urban, Rural+Urban
- **Categories:** 27 product categories
- **Granularity:** Monthly data

## Tools Used
- **Microsoft Excel** (Data Cleaning, Analysis, Dashboard)

## Analysis Performed

### 1. CPI Basket Analysis (May 2023)
Analysed broader category contributions to the CPI basket.
- Identified highest contributing categories
- Pan, Tobacco and Intoxicants showed highest weight at 9.3%

### 2. Year-over-Year Inflation Trend (2017–2023)
Tracked annual inflation rates with key event annotations.
- Identified peak inflation year
- 2019 recorded the highest inflation at 7.74%
- Mapped key drivers for each year

### 3. Food Inflation Analysis
Deep-dive into 12-month food inflation trends.
- Identified biggest category contributor to food inflation
- Spices recorded the largest increase at +30.9 points
- Analysed food price volatility across rural and urban sectors

### 4. COVID-19 Impact Analysis
Compared pre-lockdown vs post-lockdown inflation across key categories.
- Measured inflation spikes caused by supply chain disruptions
- Post-COVID general index increased by +9.4%
- Identified categories most impacted by pandemic disruptions

### 5. Oil Price Correlation Analysis
Studied relationship between Brent crude oil prices and CPI category movements.
- Fuel & Light showed the strongest positive correlation at +0.548
- Identified categories most sensitive to oil price changes
- Transport and Communication showed secondary correlation effects

## Key Insights

**Insight 1 — Highest CPI Weight:**
> Pan, Tobacco and Intoxicants contributed the highest weight to the CPI basket at 9.3% as of May 2023.

**Insight 2 — Peak Inflation Year:**
> 2019 recorded the highest inflation rate at 7.74% driven by food and fuel price pressures.

**Insight 3 — Food Inflation Driver:**
> Spices was the biggest driver of food inflation with a +30.9 point increase — the largest movement across all food sub-categories.

**Insight 4 — COVID Impact:**
> Post-COVID disruptions caused the general CPI index to increase by +9.4% driven by supply chain breakdowns and demand-supply imbalances across food and fuel categories.

**Insight 5 — Oil Price Sensitivity:**
> Fuel & Light showed the strongest correlation with Brent crude oil prices at +0.548, confirming that oil price fluctuations are a primary driver of energy-related CPI movements in India.

**Insight 6 — Rural vs Urban:**
> Urban inflation was consistently higher than Rural inflation in food and fuel categories, reflecting the higher dependency of urban households on market-driven prices.
